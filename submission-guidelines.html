Authorship

Authorship Criteria

JES has adopted the ICMJE definition of authorship and contribution. JES expects that each individual listed as an author of a manuscript has made substantive intellectual contributions to the manuscript and qualifies for authorship, and everyone who qualifies for authorship has been included in the authorship list. To qualify for authorship, an author must meet all the following criteria:

  1. Substantial contributions to conception and design, or acquisition of data, or analysis and interpretation of data;
  2. Drafting the article or revising it critically for important intellectual content;
  3. Final approval of the version to be published; and
  4. Agreement to be accountable for all aspects of the work in ensuring that questions related to the accuracy or integrity of any part of the work are appropriately investigated and resolved.

Authors are responsible for determining authorship. The Journal of Evidence Synthesis will not enter into authorship-related discussions or arbitrate authorship disputes. All authors should collectively determine who is to be an author and an acknowledged contributor, and finalize the authorship list before submitting their manuscript to the journal. Authorship implies responsibility and accountability for the manuscript content as well as the integrity and validity of the work submitted.

Authors submitting to JES must attach a cover letter to the submission signed by the corresponding author confirming that:

  1. The submitted manuscript is original work and no part has been published or submitted elsewhere.
  2. All authors have read and approved the submitted version of the manuscript.
  3. The development of the manuscript has adhered to ethical standards.
  4. All authors have contributed to the development of the ideas, writing, and/or final review of the submitted manuscript.

All authors entered into Editorial Manager® during the submission of a manuscript to JES receive an email asking them to accept or decline authorship for the submission to ensure they are aware and approve of the submission, its content, and the responsibilities associated with authorship. Upon registering in Editorial Manager®, authors are automatically assigned a peer reviewer role, and may be contacted by JES to peer review manuscripts related to their field.

Artificial Intelligence Authoring Tools Authorship Policy

The journal does not consider Artificial Intelligence authoring tools to meet the requirements for Authorship as recommended by the ICMJE. The use of such tools must be included in the article's Declarations section.

Authors who use AI tools in the writing of a manuscript, production of images or graphical elements of the paper, or in the collection and analysis of data, must be transparent in disclosing in the Materials and Methods (or similar section) of the paper how the AI tool was used and which tool was used. Authors are fully responsible for the content of their manuscript, even those parts produced by an AI tool, and are thus liable for any breach of publication ethics.

In addition, authors should not upload an accepted or published manuscript or any part of it into a generative AI tool as this may violate the copyright agreement or licensing terms in effect at the time of acceptance.

Contributors and Acknowledgments

Individuals who have contributed to the development of a manuscript but do not meet all four ICMJE criteria for authorship should not be listed in the authorship list. Honorary, guest, or ghost authorship is considered unethical and any instances or allegations of such practice will be investigated in line with COPE recommendations. Examples of contributions that do not, on their own, justify authorship include funding acquisition, data collection, general supervision of the research, writing assistance, language editing, and proofreading. Individuals who are contributors should be recognized in the "Acknowledgments" section of the manuscript alongside a specific description of their contribution. It is expected that authors have obtained written permission from acknowledged individuals to be named. Institutions and organizations should not be listed in the acknowledgments section without specifying an individual who warrants acknowledgment.

Corresponding Author Responsibilities

The corresponding author represents and speaks on behalf of all coauthors and takes sole responsibility for communicating with the journal and managing correspondence between the authorship team. The corresponding author must be available to promptly answer any queries from the journal during the editorial and production process. They are also responsible for responding to any requests, critiques, or questions about their published manuscript, and should inform all coauthors of any matters or issues that arise after publication.

The corresponding author is responsible for ensuring the submitted manuscript meets the editorial and publishing policies for The Journal Evidence Synthesis. This includes ensuring that consensus is reached amongst all authors on the inclusion and order of authors on the manuscript's authorship list; that all authors have agreed to be listed as an author, have read and approved the final version of the manuscript, and its submission to the journal; and that all authors have seen the final author proof of the manuscript before it is published. The corresponding author has ultimate responsibility for ensuring that the content of the author proof, including the spelling of author names and affiliations, is accurate prior to publication.

Change in Authorship

JES expects that authors carefully consider and finalize the inclusion and order of authors before submitting their manuscript to the journal. Any changes to the order of the authors listed, or the deletion or addition of authors, must be made before the manuscript has been accepted for publication and must be approved by the Journal. To request approval, the corresponding author must send a formal letter that explains the reason for the change in authorship and includes the manuscript details, the new authorship list, and signed confirmation from all authors listed on the original submission, including those whose names have been removed or added, that they agree to the change in authorship.

The editor-in-chief may seek confirmation of these changes from one or more of the authors or contributors as required. A change in authorship will only be granted where a valid reason has been provided and all authors agree with the change. A manuscript will not be published until such a letter has been received by the The Journal of Evidence Synthesis editorial office. No changes to the authorship list can be made after acceptance or publication of the manuscript. A change to the authorship list after publication will only be considered in exceptional circumstances and, if approved by the editor-in-chief, will be made through the publication of a corrigendum in a subsequent issue (and linked to the original article).

Open Access

The Journal of Evidence Synthesis adopts a hybrid publishing model, meaning it publishes both subscription-based as well as free and open access articles. Authors of accepted peer-reviewed systematic or scoping reviews, methodology papers, and discussion papers have the choice to pay a fee to allow perpetual, unrestricted online access to their published article to readers globally, immediately upon publication. Authors may take advantage of the open access option at the point of acceptance to ensure that this choice has no influence on the peer review and acceptance process. These articles are subject to the journal's standard peer-review process and will be accepted or rejected based on their own merit. The article processing fee is charged on acceptance of the article and should be paid within 30 days by the author, funding agency, or institution. Payment must be processed for the article to be published as open access. For pricing, please visit our webpage. All systematic and scoping review protocols are available as "free" access.

Please note: protocols are automatically published as free access, which differs from open access in that the publisher retains copyright.

Creative Commons Licence

Open access articles are freely available to read, download, and share from the time of publication. Articles are published under the terms of the Creative Commons License Attribution-NonCommercial No Derivative (CC BY-NC-ND) license, which allows readers to disseminate and reuse the article. This license does not permit reuse for any commercial purposes, nor does it cover the reuse or modification of individual elements of the work (such as figures, tables, etc.) in the creation of derivative works without specific permission.

Authors whose work is funded by certain organizations that require it will be able to publish under the terms of the Creative Commons Attribution (CC BY) license.

Compliance with Funder-Mandated Open Access Policies

The open access option provides authors with a means to comply with open access mandates and meet applicable license requirements. More information on pricing, the Creative Commons licenses, and compliance with the public access and open access policies can be found here.

Manuscript Style and Preparation Guidelines

Manuscripts submitted to the journal must meet the following requirements to be considered for peer review:

  • Adhere to the relevant reporting guidelines
  • Register the review in PROSPERO, OSF as per the review registration guide
  • Use continuous line numbers in the submitted manuscript
  • Meet JES's standards for English language as per JES submission guidelines
  • Contain the essential elements for a protocol or review
  • Adhere to requirements around masking of authors' names and affiliations
  • Use appropriate tone and language

Authors of manuscripts under consideration will be required to reformat the document before it is accepted, and authors will be notified when this is required. These final requirements are described below under Manuscript Formatting.

Cover Letter

Authors are required to upload a cover letter as part of their submission. The cover letter should include, at a minimum, the following statements that:

  • The manuscript is original work and that no part of the manuscript has been published or submitted elsewhere for publication
  • All authors have read and approved this version of the manuscript and its submission to the journal
  • The development of the submitted manuscript has adhered to ethical standards
  • Authors contributed to the development of the ideas, writing, and/or final review of the submitted manuscript
Title Page

Authors are required to upload a separate title page as part of their submission. Do not include authors' names and affiliations in the body of the main manuscript.

The title page must include:

  • A complete manuscript title (25 words or less): the title should also appear in the body of the manuscript
  • Authors' full names: do not include position titles or post-nominal qualifications
  • Each author's institutional affiliation(s) in the following format: school/faculty/research center, university, city, state, country OR department, organization, city, state (abbreviated), country
  • The full name of the corresponding author and their email address: note: only 1 corresponding author is permitted per manuscript; the corresponding author should be the person uploading the submission to Editorial Manager
  • Acknowledgments, funding, declarations, author contributions, availability of data, and conflict of interest: included on the title page only and not in the manuscript

An example of the correct format for the title page can be found here.

PRISMA 2020 Checklist

All systematic reviews must provide a completed 27-item PRISMA checklist upon submission. Scoping reviews must provide a completed 22-item PRISMA-ScR checklist upon submission. Checklists and resources via the following links:

  • PRISMA checklist
  • PRISMA extension for Scoping Reviews (PRISMA-ScR)
Manuscript Style and Formatting

The manuscript must be submitted as a Microsoft Word document (.doc, .docx)

Abbreviations/Acronyms/Initialisms
  • Use sparingly and only to reduce repetition of long, technical terms
  • Do not create acronyms that are identical to other well-known acronyms with other established meanings
  • Write out the full term for each abbreviation at its first use, followed by the abbreviation in parentheses, unless it is a standard unit of measure; thereafter, use the abbreviation only
  • Can be used in tables and figures, providing they are defined in the legend/caption
Direct Quotations

Other than for review findings and illustrations in qualitative reviews, direct quotations should be used sparingly. Use double quote marks (American style) for direct quotations (eg, "double quote marks") and include the page number (eg, (p.2)) or paragraph number (eg, (para.2) if you are citing an online source). Restrict the use of direct quotations to definitions; theories, laws, regulations, or principles; specific terms or expressions created by cited authors; or significant statements from seminal works.

Drugs and Therapeutic Agents

These should be referred to by their generic names, in lower case (eg, aspirin). If proprietary drugs have been used in the study, refer to these by their generic name, mentioning the proprietary name, and the name and location of the manufacturer in parentheses.

Inclusive Language

The Journal of Evidence Synthesis encourages authors to consider using inclusive language (eg, person-first terms, non-gendered language). In some cases, it may be appropriate to include a disclaimer (eg, We have used the term women to mean a person assigned a female designation at birth. We recognize that not all people designated female at birth identify as women.). Further guidance can be found at the Conscious Style Guide.

Language

American English spelling.

Line Numbers

Add continuous line numbers to the text (ie, do not restart the numbering on each page), which can be activated in Microsoft Word (Layout > Line Numbers > Continuous).

Measurements

All measurements must be presented in standard international (SI) units.

Numbers

Use numbers (not words) throughout the manuscript, except when a sentence starts with a number or it is part of a proper noun. Appendices should use Roman numerals (eg, I, II, III). Units of measurement should be in Arabic numerals (eg, 5 mg, 4 km).

Citations and References

The Journal of Evidence Synthesis uses a modified Vancouver, numbered citation style. Authors should format references in accordance with the guidance listed below, and in all other cases follow the standards outlined in the National Library of Medicine's Citing Medicine. We recommend the use of a tool such as EndNote or Zotero for reference management and formatting. An EndNote output style file that can be installed in EndNote is available for download here. Authors are responsible for the accuracy of the references.

  • Include a maximum of 30 references for protocols
  • All references should be numbered in the same consecutive order as they appear in the text
  • List the first 6 authors followed by et al
  • Commas separate each author's name—do not use "and" to separate the last two author names
  • Abbreviate journal names according to the style used in PubMed. The correct abbreviation to use is the NLM title abbreviation (eg, J Evid Synth)
  • Include the year, volume, issue, and first and last page numbers for journal articles
  • All online resources must include the date cited in the following format: for example, [cited 2020 Jan 23]
  • Do not include the month of publication, DOI, or PMID numbers
Journal Articles

Author(s). Article title. Abbreviated journal name Year;volume(issue):page nos.

Example: Munn Z, Barker TH, Moola S, Tufanaru C, Stern C, McArthur A, et al. Methodological quality of case series studies: an introduction to the JBI critical appraisal tool. JBI Evid Synth 2020;18(10):2127-33.

Websites

Author(s). Webpage title [internet]. Publisher; Year [date cited]. Available from: [website link].

Example: Aromataris E, Lockwood C, Porritt K, Pilla B, Jordan Z, editors. JBI Manual for Evidence Synthesis [internet]. JBI; 2024 [cited 2025 Mar 4]. Available from: https://synthesismanual.jbi.global.

Books

Author(s). Chapter title. In: Editor(s), editors. Book title, edition. Publisher; year.

Example: Levett-Jones T. Chapter 1: Clinical reasoning: what it is and why it matters. In: Levett-Jones T, editor. Clinical reasoning: learning to think like a nurse, 2nd ed. Pearson; 2018.

Reference or Review Management Software

Please ensure that the version of the software used is included in text (as per the examples shown here). Do not add a software citation to the reference list; manufacturers are included in the body of the text.

  • EndNote vX.X (Clarivate Analytics, PA, USA)
  • RevMan vX.X (Copenhagen: The Nordic Cochrane Centre, Cochrane)
  • Mendeley vX.X (Mendeley Ltd., Elsevier, Netherlands)
In-Text Citations

In-text citations should be superscripted numbers, usually at the end of a sentence. Citation numbers should be placed after full stops and commas, but before colons and semicolons. Each source is allocated a unique reference number based on the order in which it appears in the text, and that same number is used each time that reference is used.

Example: However, the use of a transparent systematic process to identify the best available textual evidence can reduce publication bias through evidence that might not otherwise be disseminated within peer-reviewed publications,1 and provide practical guidance to practitioners and policymakers. Textual evidence provides additional sources of important data from ongoing research and a forum for disseminating all evidence findings by drawing on the expertise and experience of individuals and organizations across a wide range of disciplines (ie, health care, education, law, journalism, socio-political, implementation science, and evaluation).2

Tables, Figures, and Supplemental Content
  • If a table or figure is not the authors' own work, the appropriate permissions must be acquired by the authors and a copy of the permission to use the material submitted to the journal. Appropriate acknowledgments should be included as a footnote to the table or figure
  • Authors are responsible for any costs associated with acquiring permission to reproduce third-party content
  • Appendices do not need to be submitted as separate files
  • The use of supplemental digital content (SDC) will be at the discretion of the Editorial Office. Authors will be advised if it is deemed necessary as part of the publication process
  • Figures and tables must be referred to in the text (eg, "as shown in Table 1")
  • Figure and table numbering: Tables must be numbered using Arabic numerals (eg, Table 1, Table 2) and cited consecutively in the manuscript
  • Figure and table titles must be in sentence case (eg, Figure 1: Search results and study selection and inclusion process) and must be placed above the figure. They should be concise and informative and, with an accompanying caption if necessary, provide enough detail to allow interpretation of the figure without reference to the body of the text
  • Include a legend with definitions of any symbols used and define/explain all abbreviations and units of measurement in the figure title in parentheses. Footnote symbols: a,b,c,d should be used and *, **, *** reserved for P-values
Tables
  • Tables should be self-contained and complement, not duplicate, information contained in the text
  • Table column headings should be brief, with units of measurement in parentheses
Figures
  • All illustrations (line drawings and photographs) are classified as figures
  • The PRISMA flow diagram must be submitted as an editable Word document—not as an image or PDF
Figure Specifications
  • Figures should be sized to fit within the column (82mm) or the full text width (173mm)
  • Crop out any white or black space surrounding the image
  • If relevant, magnifications should be indicated using a scale bar on the illustration
  • Artwork should be saved as TIFF, EPS, or MS Office (DOC, PPT, XLS) files. High-resolution PDF files are also acceptable
  • Diagrams, drawings, graphs, and other line art must be vector or saved at a resolution of at least 1200 dpi. If created in an MS Office program, send the original (DOC, PPT, XLS) file
  • Photographs, radiographs, and other halftone images must be saved at a resolution of at least 300 dpi
  • Photographs and radiographs with text must be saved as postscript or at a resolution of at least 600 dpi
